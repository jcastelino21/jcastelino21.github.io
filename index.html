BC/AD and BCE/CE

BCE/CE and BC/AD

Overview

BC/AD and BCE/CE help us place events in chronological order. BC and BCE are the same, and AD/CE are also the same.
BC/AD BCE/CE
BC stands for 'Before Christ' BCE stands for 'Before Common Era'
AD stands for 'Anno Domini' ('Year of the Lord') CE stands for 'Common Era'
Timeline is based around the brith of Jesus Christ Timeline is secular, which means it is not based on religion
Current years are labeled as AD We live in the Common Era

Key Points

• The year "Zero" does not exist


• For BCE/BC: The higher the number, the EARLIER the event happened. Example: The year 1000 BCE happened BEFORE the year 500 BCE


• For AD/BCE: The higher the number, the LATER the event happened. Example: The year 2000 CE happened AFTER the year 1000 CE


• When discussing modern times in the common era, 'ce' is often assumed Example: The year 2018 can be simplified to just 2018


How To Determine Age

• If both numbers are in BCE/BC: Take the number of the older event, (the larger number) and SUBTRACT the more recent event (the smaller number). Example: 545 BCE - 445 BCE = 100 Years


• If both numbers are in AD/BCE: Take the number of the more recent event, (the larger number) and SUBTRACT the older event (the smaller number) Example: 2010 - 1910 = 100 Years
